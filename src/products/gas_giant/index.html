Gas Giant Exploration

Day 1

Our journey begins at Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system. Using advanced propulsion systems, we will enter orbit around the planet and have the opportunity to study its famous Great Red Spot and observe the colorful bands of clouds that circle the planet.

Day 13

Next, we will visit Io, the most volcanically active object in the solar system. Using specialized spacecraft, we will safely fly through the intense radiation belts and approach the moon's surface to witness volcanic eruptions and explore volcanic caldera.

Day 44

Our final destination is Titan, Saturn's largest moon, which has a thick atmosphere and a unique methane cycle. We will use landers to explore the surface and study the moon's geology, atmosphere, and potential for life.

Day 50

Arrival back on Earth! Reflect on the amazing sights and discoveries made on this incredible journey to the planets. Share your findings with the world and plan your next space adventure!

Day 21

Afterwards we will travel to Europa, one of Jupiter's icy moons, scientists believe that beneath its icy crust there is a subsurface ocean, we will fly over its icy surface, using special equipments to scan the moon for signs of potential subsurface water and the potential for life.

Day 31

Afterwards we will travel to Saturn, the second largest planet in our solar system and famous for its rings. We will fly through the rings and get a close-up view of the planet's diverse moons, including Titan, which we will explore next.
