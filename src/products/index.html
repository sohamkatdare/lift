Terrestrial Planet Exploration

Day 1

Depart Earth and begin the journey to Mercury, the planet closest to the sun. The trip will take about 3.5 months, so be sure to bring plenty of supplies and entertainment.

Day 10

Arrival at Mercury! The surface temperature here can reach up to 800 degrees Fahrenheit, so be sure to wear a protective suit. Explore the planet's rocky, cratered terrain and take measurements of the thin atmosphere.

Day 24

Depart Venus and begin the journey to Mars, the fourth planet from the sun. The trip will take about 6 months.

Day 44

Arrival at Mars! The surface temperature here is much cooler than on Venus or Mercury, but it can still reach up to 70 degrees Fahrenheit in the daytime. Explore the planet's reddish, rocky terrain and search for signs of past or present life. Investigate the erosion marks of past water. Take readings of the thin atmosphere and study the planet's polar ice caps. Take note of the amazing landmarks, like Olympus Mons, the solar systemâ€™s tallest volcano.



Day 12

Depart Mercury and begin the journey to Venus, the second planet from the sun. The trip will take about 2 months.

Day 22

Arrival at Venus! The surface temperature here is hot enough to melt lead, so again, be sure to wear a protective suit. Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere, but our protective suits will make sure that you do not inhale any of it or get crushed under the pressure. Take readings of the atmosphere and study the volcanoes on the surface.

Day 46

Depart Mars and begin the journey back to Earth. The trip will take about 6 months.

Day 60

Arrival back on Earth! Reflect on the amazing sights and discoveries made on this incredible journey to the planets. Share your findings with the world and plan your next space adventure!

Gas Giant Exploration

Day 1

Our journey begins at Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system. Using advanced propulsion systems, we will enter orbit around the planet and have the opportunity to study its famous Great Red Spot and observe the colorful bands of clouds that circle the planet.

Day 13

Next, we will visit Io, the most volcanically active object in the solar system. Using specialized spacecraft, we will safely fly through the intense radiation belts and approach the moon's surface to witness volcanic eruptions and explore volcanic caldera.

Day 44

Our final destination is Titan, Saturn's largest moon, which has a thick atmosphere and a unique methane cycle. We will use landers to explore the surface and study the moon's geology, atmosphere, and potential for life.

Day 50

Arrival back on Earth! Reflect on the amazing sights and discoveries made on this incredible journey to the planets. Share your findings with the world and plan your next space adventure!

Day 21

Afterwards we will travel to Europa, one of Jupiter's icy moons, scientists believe that beneath its icy crust there is a subsurface ocean, we will fly over its icy surface, using special equipments to scan the moon for signs of potential subsurface water and the potential for life.

Day 31

Afterwards we will travel to Saturn, the second largest planet in our solar system and famous for its rings. We will fly through the rings and get a close-up view of the planet's diverse moons, including Titan, which we will explore next.
