Preparing you for the trip of your life.

Training makes sure you are safe during your long voyage through alien environments.

What is the point of training?



The training required of our customers is designed to be challenging but intuitive to prepare them for the demands
of our wonderful trip. Lift space training, also known as SpaceCamp™️, is a 4-week program that takes place at the Galileo facilities
in San Diego, California, or Parris Island, South Carolina. The training includes physical conditioning, cardiovascular training,
and instruction in our unique and innovative Lift spacecraft. After completing SpaceCamp™️, some individuals may receive additional training.
This training is to make sure everyone is ready for the exciting adventure ahead. This training may be technical, such as learning how to operate
and function within space, but the goal of this mandatory training is to ensure that our customers have a better experience during their LIFT trip.

Fitness screening



Fitness screening for space travelers is a crucial step in ensuring the safety and success of space missions. The screening process aims to identify any potential health risks that could arise from exposure to the unique environment of space, such as microgravity and radiation. These risks can include cardiovascular issues, bone and muscle loss, and changes in vision. Additionally, the screening process evaluates the overall physical and mental health of the individual, including their fitness level, medical history, and any pre-existing conditions. As astronaut Clayton Anderson once said, "You cannot afford to be sick in space. It's not like being on Earth. Spaceflight can be detrimental to your health. You must be in optimal physical condition." Therefore, the fitness screening process plays a critical role in ensuring that space travelers are prepared to handle the physical and mental demands of space travel.

Basic Training



The next step of space training will include basic space travel training. Space tourists will need to receive basic training in the principles of space travel, including the effects of microgravity on the body and safety protocols. The participants need to be ready for the changes that space has on the body. Additionally, the participants will go through a mission-specific training in which they will receive special training for the particular mission/package they choose. Space tourists will need to know how to operate any equipment or perform any tasks that may be required during their space travel experience.

Emergency Training



In the third stage of training, we will train the participants to be ready in space and how its environment. This includes different simulations that will portray different possible scenarios in the case of an emergency. It's important that space tourists know how to respond in the event of an emergency while in space. Training may include simulations of various emergency scenarios and drills to practice emergency procedures. This emergency training consists of different simulations that depict a real-like scene in which the tourist must be able perform the correct duties in the case of an emergency.

Zero gravity



In the last part of the training, the tourists will undergo more simulations that depict the gravity-less environment of space. This last part of the training will include a full review of the training program as well as a rehearsal of the actual launch. This will ensure that all the participants are ready for the launch on its specified date.
